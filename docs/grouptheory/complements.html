1. Schur-Zassenhaus

This section is devoted to the proof of the following powerful theorem:

Theorem 1.1: Schur-Zassenhaus

Let $G$ be finite and $N \lhd_b G$ of order $a$.

  1. If $(a,b) = 1$, then there exists a complementary subgroup of order $b$.
  2. If, moreover, $N$ or $G/N$ is soluble, then all such subgroups are conjugated.

In fact, it was proven in 1963 by Feit and Thompson that all finite groups of odd order are soluble, which means that the solubility assumption in (2) is actually superfluous. However, the proof is 254 pages long and we don't want to rely on it in this short summary.
